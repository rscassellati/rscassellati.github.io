Project Explanation

Paired with Jon Schlesinger, Executive Director of the Hiatt Brandeis Career Center, I worked to revolutionize the way that the Career Center interacted with and used data to make data-informed decision making as part of the DSII (Data Science Internal Internship).

The DSII was a high level presidential initiative to "drive innovation and increase operational effectiveness" in higher education. Learn more about the DSII project.

Over the span of 8 months, I worked in a scrum model to iteratively develop new and innovative ways to visualize complex and large data. This project cumulated in a website with 7 main types of graphs, 3 different ways to restrict the data, and 9 different customization options.

My work spanned far beyond just the coding of the project, as I met weekly with the client Jon Schlesinger and turned conceptual ideas into software, problem solving and working creatively to resolve challenges as they arose.

This work required two key skills, the first being the ability to understand and incorporate business needs and desired outcomes from a client, translating that into the data science and technological work that is necessary to accomplish those goals. The second skill I relied heavily on throughout this process was the ability to teach myself new tools, techniques, and technologies to accomplish whatever was needed to create the best output for the project.

Please note that due to data security and the sensitive nature of the data being handled, full access isn't possible for this project. To fully explore a project, check out some of my other work doing information visualization here.
